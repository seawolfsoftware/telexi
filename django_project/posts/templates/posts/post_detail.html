The following images are visual representations of Kanzi, a 40 year-old bonobo who lives in Iowa.

Kanzi knows the Chinese-ish character below as his name in lexigram form, designed by sapien Sue Savage-Rumbaugh.

Image three is a drawing Kanzi produced of his own lexigram.

If the purpose of a key within a dictionary is to map a name to a value, then perhaps life begins with an empty dictionary, adding new keys only when encountering new values through experience with the environment or abstract thought.

Memory retention of keys would depend on repeated interactions or rethinkings of the value, motivating the discoverer to strengthen the association between the two, if not for the sake of communication then for convenience.

This idea of constructing an input-output map is common in software, where the algorithm receives labeled data as pairs and the goal is to learn the relationship between X and Y.

Equipped with his keys, Kanzi has been able to share an immeasurable wealth of ideas to sapiens, mainly to Sue, a life companion since being only months old.

But what if Sue never gave Kanzi a keyboard ? ? ?
