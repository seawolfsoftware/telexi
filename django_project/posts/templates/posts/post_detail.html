A special bonobo



The following images are visual representations of Kanzi, a 40 year-old bonobo who lives in Iowa. Kanzi knows the Chinese-like character below as his name in lexigram form, designed by sapien Sue Savage-Rumbaugh.

The third image is a drawing Kanzi produced of his own lexigram.

Equipped with his keys Kanzi has been able to share an immeasurable wealth of ideas with sapiens. He forms novel compounds from existing lexigrams, having insightfully referenced kale as "slow lettuce".

Most significantly, Sue's methods have enabled Kanzi to become an active participant in the Pan Homo culture shaped by sapiens.

If the purpose of a key within a dictionary is to map a name to a value, then perhaps lifeforms begin with an empty dictionary and add new keys only when encountering new values through experiences with the environment or abstract thought.

Memory retention of keys would depend on repeated interactions or rethinkings of the value, strengthening the association between the two, if not for the sake of communication then for convenience.

Constructing an input-output map is common in software. An algorithm receives labeled data as pairs and the goal is to learn the relationship between X and Y.

I can't help but think ... what if Kanzi never received a keyboard ?
