enabling expression through experiment

Dogs communicate through an array
of verbal and non-verbal signals like
body posture, facial expressions, and tail wagging.

Deciphering these signals is often challenging for humans.
Is a dog really happy when its tail wags?
We think so, but the answer lies in the situation's context
and requires human interpretation, a faulty approach at best.

Dog communication buttons

for machine learning

coming Spring 2021

telexi (telephone + lexigram) is a new platform
which employs touch signals to improve communication
between humans and dogs.

When a dog touches a tile with its nose or paw,
telexi interprets the touch as an intent
and vocalizes the intent with an audio clip.

How it works

Let's consider a trainer who has conditioned his dog
to associate the word water with the real thing.

When he refills his dog's water he speaks the word aloud
so the dog knows the name "water" by reference.


But knowing a word alone isn't very useful.
Communication only flows one-way.

The trainer can speak "water",
but the dog remains unable to utilize
its understanding of the name independently.

Now let's equip the trainer with a telexi device and online profile.

Enable Wifi
  • Power on device
  • Open Wifi settings on mobile device
  • Select telexi network
  • Device is online
Create Profile
  • Go to telexi.io/activate
  • Enter device's serial number
  • Profile created
Record Word
  • Go to telexi.io/profile
  • Record word prompt appears
  • Record word
  • telexi uploads word to device

The trainer can now attach a tile to represent the word water.

In order to distinguish between words, each tile must be unique.
To accomplish this we use shapes called lexigrams.

The shape of his dog's water dish is a circle,
so the trainer selects a circle among the lexigram decals
and connects the lexigram to the device at one of 10 ports.

Ok, the dog can “talk” but isn’t this a novelty?

True -- touching a tile to speak a word isn’t exactly remarkable.
Products like these exist already.

What separates telexi
from traditional dog communication buttons
is the integration of software.

telexi's profiles track touch events over time to
visualize patterns which emerge in data.

Data is continuously streamed and sourced
as a reference for dogs of the same
breed, sex, age, and other criteria.

telexi's development was inspired by the research of John W. Pilley, whose work with Chaser the Border Collie demonstrates that dogs can comprehend sentences with a prepositional object, verb, and direct object.

(Pilley recounts their triumphs and shortcomings with lexicon in Chaser: Unlocking the Genius of the Dog Who Knows a Thousand Words ).

Many academics like Pilley have published research on dog communication and perception. Their findings have informed our sense of what's possible in canine signals.

seawolf software thinks dogs are capable of abstract, complex thought. As a work in progress, we aim to develop telexi as a tool to better enable dogs to express these thoughts.

In doing so, we hope to accelerate humanity's understanding of dog cognition, sourced by touch events from our best friends around the world.
