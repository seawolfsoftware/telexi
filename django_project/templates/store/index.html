telexi (telephone + lexigram) is a platform to improve communication between humans and dogs.

Physically, telexi is a series of tiles with touch sensors and a speaker.

When a dog touches a tile with its nose or paw, telexi interprets this touch as an intent and vocalizes the intent with an audio clip.

How it works

Let's consider an trainer who's conditioned his dog to associate the word water with the real thing.

When he gives his dog water, he speaks "water" out loud
so the dog knows the name by reference.


But knowing a word alone isn't so useful. The communication only flows one-way.

The trainer can speak "water", but the dog can't.
It remains unable to request water.

Now let's equip the trainer with a telexi hub and mobile app.

Connect hub to Wifi
  • plug hub into power outlet
  • open mobile device's Wifi settings
  • select telexi network
  • hub connected to Wifi
Record word
  • open telexi app
  • prompt displays to record word
  • record word/re-record word
  • Submit recorded word
  • telexi uploads word to hub

The trainer can now attach a tile to represent the word water.

In order to distinguish between words, each tile must be unique. To accomplish this, we use shapes called lexigrams.

The shape of his dog's water dish is a circle, so the trainer chooses a circle and connects the lexigram to the hub at port 1.

Ok, so the dog can “talk” but isn’t this a novelty?

True -- touching a tile to speak a word isn’t exactly remarkable. Products like these exist already.

What separates telexi from traditional dog communication buttons is the integration of software.

telexi's app tracks touch events over time to visualize patterns which emerge in data.

The data is streamed, profiled and sourced as a reference point for dogs of the same breed, sex, age, or other criteria.

telexi's development was inspired by the research of John W. Pilley, whose work with Chaser the Border Collie demonstrates that dogs can comprehend sentences with a prepositional object, verb, and direct object. (Pilley recounts their triumphs and shortcomings in Chaser: Unlocking the Genius of the Dog Who Knows a Thousand Words ).

Many other academics like Pilley have studied and published research on dog communication and perception. Their findings have informed our sense of what's possible with canine signals and lexicon.

seawolf software believes dogs are capable of complex and abstract thought. As a work in progress, we aim to develop telexi as a tool to better enable dogs to express these thoughts.

In doing so, we hope to accelerate humanity's understanding of dog cognition, sourced from touch events by our own best friends around the world.
