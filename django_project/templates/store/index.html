enabling expression through experiment

A compound of telephone and lexigram, telexi is a communication platform which seeks to improve mutual understanding between humans and dogs.

What is it ...exactly?

Physically, telexi is a series of manufactured tiles with embedded touch sensors and a speaker.

The sensors enable telexi to receive and process touch events from dogs.

When a dog touches a tile with its nose or paw, telexi interprets this touch as an intent and vocalizes the intent with an associated audio clip.

How does it work?

Consider a dog who’s conditioned to associate the word “water” with the real thing. When its trainer speaks “water”, the dog receives water.

Now let's equip the trainer with a telexi device and smartphone app.

The trainer plugs the device into a power outlet. He then opens his phone's wifi settings, and selects the telexi network, establishing an internet connection with his local wifi router.

Now the trainer opens the telexi app, where he's prompted for a recording. He speaks "water" and submits the audio clip.

Our trainer is now ready to attach a tile to the device to serve as a visual representation of "water".

In order to distinguish between words, we must use unique shapes called lexigrams. Because the shape of his dog's water dish is a circle, he chooses that shape and connects the tile to the device.

Navigating back to the app, he assigns the "water" audio clip to the tile.

telexi uploads the clip into his device's vocabulary, which can now speak "water" from its position on the wall, floor, or surface.

Now, when the dog touches the tile, it can request water! The dog telephoned a lexigram!

Ok, so the dog can “talk” but isn’t this a novelty?

True -- touching a tile to speak a word isn’t exactly remarkable. Products like these exist already.


What separates telexi from traditional training buttons is the integration of software.

telexi's app tracks touch events over time to visualize patterns which emerge in data.

The data is profiled and sourced as a reference point for dogs of the same breed, sex, age, or other criteria.

telexi's development was inspired by the research of John W. Pilley, whose work with Chaser the Border Collie demonstrates that dogs can comprehend sentences with a prepositional object, verb, and direct object. For example, "take snoopy To boston" from Trial 2a. Read here

We believe dogs are capable of even more complex and abstract thought and we aim to develop a tool to enable dogs to express these thoughts.

In doing so, we wish to offer access to data for researchers like the late John Pilley in order to accelerate humanity's understanding of dog cognition, sourced from touch events by our own best friends around the world.
